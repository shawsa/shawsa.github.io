RBF-Interpolation

Sat Oct 6 17:38:32 2018

Given a set of distinct points $\{\vec{x}_i\}_{i=1}^n \subseteq D$ and function values $\{f_i\}_{i=1}^n \subseteq \RR$ (or $\CC$) at those points an interpolant $s : D \to \RR$ is a function such that $s(\vec{x}_i) = f_i$ for $i=1, 2, ..., n$. This article discusses RBF interpolation and compares it to polynomial interpolation.

Projection Method

Sun Sep 23 01:33:01 2018

Radial Basis Function Finite Difference Methods rely on the calculation of the linear opperator applied to the RBF ($\LL \phi$) to be independent of position. This is not the case for many surface operators. For these, one needs a method that accounts for the local geometry of the surface in the calculation of the operator. The projection method is exactly that. This article looks at using the projection method along with iterated differentiation to approximate the surface Laplacian $\Delta_\SS$.

Experiment 003 RBF Independent Error

Sat Sep 1 17:46:26 2018

It has been obsereved that when using iterated differentiation to solve the problem $\Delta_\SS u = -20Y_{4,-3}$ on the unit sphere, if the degree of the basis terms is 5 (i.e. one higher than the degree of the forcing function) then the error is independent of the choice of RBF. We attempt to explain this behavior.

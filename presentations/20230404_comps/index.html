Traveling wave solutions

Look for steady-state solutions in the traveling wave coordinate frame $\xi = x - ct$.

$$ \begin{align} -c\tau_u U' &= -U + \int_\mathbb{R} \frac{1}{2} e^{-|\xi - \xi'|} Q(\xi')f\big[U(\xi')\big] \ d\xi' \\ -c \tau_q Q' &= 1 - Q - \beta Q f[U]. \end{align} $$

Progressive fronts: $c > 0$, Active region $= (-\infty, 0)$. $$ \begin{align} -c\tau_u U' &= -U + \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2} e^{-|\xi - \xi'|} Q(\xi')\big] \ d\xi' \\ -c \tau_q Q' &= 1 - Q - \beta Q H(-\xi). \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align*} Q(\xi) &= \begin{cases} 1, & \xi > 0 \\ \gamma + (1- \gamma)e^{\xi/c\tau_q\gamma}, & \xi < 0 \end{cases} \\ U(\xi) &= \begin{cases} \frac{\gamma + c\tau_q\gamma}{2(1+c\tau_q\gamma)(1+c\tau_u)} e^{-\xi}, & \xi > 0 \\ \gamma + \frac{K_1}{r}e^{\xi/c\tau_q\gamma} + \frac{K_2c\tau_u}{c\tau_u-1}e^{\xi}, & \xi < 0 \end{cases} \\ K_1 &= -\frac{(1-\gamma)c\tau_q^2\gamma^2}{\tau_u(1 - c^2\tau_q^2\gamma^2)} \\ K_2 &= -\frac{1}{2c\tau_u} \bigg(\frac{(1-\gamma)c\tau_q\gamma}{1-c\tau_q\gamma} - \gamma \bigg) \\ \theta &= \frac{\gamma + c\tau_q\gamma}{2(1+c\tau_q\gamma)(1+c\tau_u)} \end{align*} $$

Pulses: Active region = $(-\Delta, 0)$. $$ \begin{align*} Q(\xi) &= \begin{cases} 1, & 0 \le \xi \\ \gamma + (1-\gamma)e^{\xi/c\gamma\tau_q }, & -\Delta \le \xi < 0 \\ 1 + \big[-1 + Q(-\Delta) \big] e^{(\xi + \Delta)/c\tau_q}, & \xi < -\Delta. \end{cases}\\ U(\xi) &= \begin{cases} A_{L} e^{\frac{\xi}{\tau_u c}} + B_{12} e^{\xi}, & \: \xi \le -\Delta \\ A_{M} e^{\frac{\xi}{c\tau_u}} + B_{1} + B_{10} e^{\xi} + B_{11} e^{\xi/c\gamma\tau_q} + B_{9} e^{- \xi}, &\: -\Delta < \xi \le 0 \\ \theta e^{- \xi}, & 0 < \xi \end{cases} \end{align*} $$

Wave response

$$ \begin{align*} u(\xi, t) &= U\big( \xi - \varepsilon \nu(t) \big) + \varepsilon \phi + \mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^2) \\ q(\xi, t) &= Q\big( \xi - \varepsilon \nu(t) \big) + \varepsilon \psi + \mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^2) \end{align*} $$
$$ \begin{align*} \tau_u u_t &= -u + w * (q f[u]) + \varepsilon I_u(x, t) \label{eqn:forced_u} \\ \tau_q q_t &= 1 - q - \beta q f[u] + \varepsilon I_q(x, t). \label{eqn:forced_q} \end{align*} $$
$$\begin{align*} M\begin{bmatrix}\phi \\ \psi \end{bmatrix}_t + \mathcal{L}\begin{bmatrix}\phi \\ \psi \end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix} I_u + \tau_u U' \nu' \\ I_q + \tau_q Q' \nu ' \end{bmatrix} \end{align*}$$ $$ \mathcal{L}(\vec{v}) = \vec{v} - cM \vec{v} + \begin{bmatrix} -w Q f'(U) * \cdot & -w f(U) * \cdot \\ \beta Q f'(U) & \beta f(U) \end{bmatrix} \vec{v} $$

Bounded solutions exist if the inhomogeneity is orthogonal to $\mathcal{N}\{\mathcal{L}\}$. For $(v_1, v_2) \in \mathcal{N}\{\mathcal{L}\}$ $$\begin{align*} -c \tau_u v_1' &= v_1 - Qf'(U) \int w(y,\xi) v_1(y) \ dy + \beta Qf'(U) v_2 \\ -c \tau_q v_2' &= v_2 - f(U) \int w(y, \xi) v_1(y) \ dy + \beta f(U) v_2. \end{align*}$$

RBF-OGr

The Radial Basis Functions Orthogonal Gradients method (RBF-OGr) originaly published in [1] is a method for solving PDEs on surfaces.

Grady outlined the basic idea in the meeting. In order to approximate the surface laplacian$\nabla^2_\SS(u)$ we note that for each point in our nodeset $\nabla^2(u) = \nabla^2_\SS(u) + \nabla^2_\vec{n}(u)$ where $\nabla^2_\vec{n}(u)$ is the component of the full laplacian $\nabla^2(u)$ in the direction of the normal vector at the particular point on the surface. The idea is to construct a function $s$ that interpolates $u$ on the surface, but for which $\nabla^2_\vec{n}(s)=0$ at each point in our node set. Once this function is obtained, the full gradient is equivalent to the surface gradient of the original function, $\nabla^2_\SS(u) = \nabla^2(s)$.

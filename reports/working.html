Advancing Traveling Front Derivation

In characteristic coordinates, the traveling front solution satisfies \begin{align*} -c\tau_u U' + U &= w \ast (Qf[U]) \\ -c\tau_q Q' + Q &= 1 - \beta Q f[U]. \end{align*} We take the active region to be $(-\infty, 0)$ and we have \begin{align*} -c\tau_u U' + U &= \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2} e^{|\xi - y|} Q(y) \ dy \\ -c\tau_q Q' + Q &= 1 - \beta Q H(-\xi). \end{align*} For $\xi \gt 0$ we enforce boundedness on $Q$ and find $Q = 1$ in this region.

For $\xi \lt 0$ our equation for $Q$ becomes \begin{align*} -c\tau_q Q' + (1+\beta)Q &= 1 \\ Q' - \frac{1}{c \gamma \tau_q} Q &= - \frac{1}{c\tau_q} \\ \left[ Q e^{-\xi/ (c \gamma \tau_q)} \right]' &= -\frac{1}{c\tau_q} e^{-\xi/ (c \gamma \tau_q)} \\ Q e^{-\xi/ (c \gamma \tau_q)} &= 1 - \frac{1}{c\tau_q} \int_0^\xi e^{-y/ (c \gamma \tau_q)} \ dy \\ &= 1 - \gamma \left(1 - e^{-\xi/(c\gamma \tau_q)} \right) \\ Q(\xi) &= \gamma + (1- \gamma) \exp \left( \frac{\xi}{c\gamma \tau_q} \right). \end{align*} Thus we have $Q$ over the entire domain \begin{align*} Q(\xi) = \begin{cases} 1, & \ \xi \ge 0, \\ \gamma + (1- \gamma) \exp \left( \frac{\xi}{c\gamma \tau_q} \right) , & \ \xi \lt 0. \end{cases} \end{align*}

Substituting into our equation for $U$, we have \begin{align*} -c\tau_u U' + U &= \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2} \left[ \gamma + (1 - \gamma) e^{y/(c\gamma\tau_q)}\right] e^{-|\xi - y|} \ dy. \end{align*}

We will evaluate the integral on the right hand side. First, consider $\xi \gt 0$. Then \begin{align*} \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2} \left[ \gamma + (1 - \gamma) e^{y/(c\gamma\tau_q)}\right] e^{-|\xi - y|} \ dy &= e^{-\xi} \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2} \left[ \gamma + (1 - \gamma) e^{y/(c\gamma\tau_q)}\right] e^{y} \ dy \\ &= e^{-\xi} \left[ \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2} \gamma e^y \ dy + \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2}(1 - \gamma) e^{y(1 + c\gamma\tau_q)/(c\gamma\tau_q)} \ dy \right] \\ &= e^{-\xi} \left[ \frac{1}{2}\gamma + \frac{1}{2}(1-\gamma) \frac{c\gamma\tau_q}{1 + c\gamma\tau_q} \right] \\ &= e^{-\xi} \frac{1}{2} \left[ \frac{\gamma + c\gamma^2 \tau_q}{1+c\gamma\tau_q} + \frac{c\gamma\tau_q - c\gamma^2\tau_q}{1+c\gamma\tau_q} \right] \\ &= e^{-\xi} \frac{1}{2} \frac{\gamma + c\gamma\tau_q}{1 + c\gamma\tau_q}. \end{align*} Then we have \begin{align*} \left[ U e^{-\xi/(c\tau_u)} \right]' &= -\frac{1}{2c\tau_u} \frac{\gamma + c\gamma\tau_q}{1 + c\gamma\tau_q} e^{-\xi (1+c\tau_u)/(c\tau_u)} \\ U e^{-\xi/(c\tau_u)} &= U_0 + \int_0^\xi -\frac{1}{2c\tau_u} \frac{\gamma + c\gamma\tau_q}{1 + c\gamma\tau_q} e^{-y (1+c\tau_u)/(c\tau_u)} \ dy \\ U e^{-\xi/(c\tau_u)} &= U_0 + \frac{1}{2c\tau_u} \frac{\gamma + c\gamma\tau_q}{1 + c\gamma\tau_q}\frac{c\tau_u}{1+c\tau_u} (e^{-\xi(1+c\tau_u)/(c\tau_u)} - 1) \\ U(\xi) &= e^{\xi/(c \tau_u)} \left( U_0 - \frac{1}{2c\tau_u} \frac{\gamma + c\gamma\tau_q}{1 + c\gamma\tau_q}\frac{c\tau_u}{1+c\tau_u} \right) + e^{-\xi} \frac{1}{2c\tau_u} \frac{\gamma + c\gamma\tau_q}{1 + c\gamma\tau_q}\frac{c\tau_u}{1+c\tau_u}. \end{align*} To enforce a bounded solution we set the $e^{\xi/(c\tau_u)}$ coefficient to zero, giving \begin{align*} U(\xi) &= \frac{\gamma + c\gamma \tau_q}{2(1+c\tau_u)(1+c\gamma\tau_q)} e^{-\xi}\\ U_0 = \theta &= \frac{\gamma + c\gamma \tau_q}{2(1+c\tau_u)(1+c\gamma\tau_q)}. \end{align*}
